About SASSA

The South African Social Security Agency (SASSA) is a government agency responsible for distributing social grants to eligible citizens. SASSA's primary objective is to ensure that all eligible citizens receive their grants in a convenient and dignified manner.

SASSA was established in 2005 as a result of the amalgamation of the former Department of Social Welfare and Population Development, the South African Social Security Agency, and the South African Agency for Social Security. The agency's primary goal is to ensure that all eligible citizens receive their social grants in a convenient and dignified manner.

Over the years, SASSA has made significant strides in improving the lives of millions of South Africans. The agency has implemented various initiatives aimed at enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of its services. One such initiative is the introduction of the biometric payment system, which has greatly reduced the risk of fraud and ensured that grants are paid directly to the beneficiaries.

As SASSA continues to evolve, the agency is committed to embracing new technologies and innovative solutions to improve its services. The agency is working towards a future where all social grants are paid electronically, reducing the need for physical cash payments and minimizing the risk of fraud. Additionally, SASSA is exploring ways to integrate its services with other government agencies to provide a more comprehensive support system for its beneficiaries.

SASSA's vision is to become a leading social security agency in Africa, recognized for its excellence in service delivery, innovation, and customer satisfaction. The agency is committed to ensuring that all eligible citizens have access to social security benefits, and that these benefits are delivered in a manner that is efficient, effective, and dignified.
